Abstract

Pixel-accurate stereo matching remains a challenging problem in computer vision, particularly when dealing with textureless regions, occlusions, and repetitive patterns. This work presents a novel approach for pixel-accurate epipolar guided matching that leverages geometric constraints to improve stereo correspondence accuracy. Our method combines deep learning with classical epipolar geometry principles to achieve state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks.

Method Overview

Our approach introduces several key innovations:

  • Epipolar-Guided Feature Matching: Novel feature matching strategy guided by epipolar constraints
  • Pixel-Accurate Refinement: Sub-pixel accurate disparity estimation through geometric optimization
  • Robust Geometric Verification: Geometric consistency checking for outlier rejection
  • End-to-End Learning: Fully trainable pipeline optimized for accuracy and efficiency
