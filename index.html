Abstract

Keypoint matching can be slow and unreliable in challenging conditions such as repetitive textures or wide-baseline views. In such cases, known geometric relations (e.g., the fundamental matrix) can be used to restrict potential correspondences to a narrow epipolar envelope, thereby reducing the search space and improving robustness. These epipolar-guided matching approaches have proved effective in tasks such as SfM; however, most rely on coarse spatial binning, which introduces approximation errors, requires costly post-processing, and may miss valid correspondences. We address these limitations with an exact formulation that performs candidate selection directly in angular space using tolerance circles and segment trees for logarithmic-time queries.

Method Overview

Our approach introduces several key innovations:

  • Epipolar-Guided Feature Matching: Novel feature matching strategy guided by epipolar constraints
  • Pixel-Accurate Refinement: Sub-pixel accurate disparity estimation through geometric optimization
  • Robust Geometric Verification: Geometric consistency checking for outlier rejection
  • Efficient Implementation: Optimized algorithm suitable for practical stereo vision applications

Results

Figure 2: Quantitative matching results demonstrating the effectiveness of our pixel-accurate epipolar guided approach.

Key Findings:

  • Consistent speedups over existing epipolar-guided matching approaches (Grid-based and Epipolar Hashing)
  • Perfect candidate recall (1.00) - recovers all valid correspondences within epipolar envelope
  • Logarithmic query complexity O(log n + k) vs linear complexity of existing methods
  • Robust performance across all 13 ETH3D sequences with varying scene complexity
  • Eliminates false positives and negatives through exact geometric formulation
  • Scalable performance: up to 10x faster candidate generation at high keypoint densities (50k points)
